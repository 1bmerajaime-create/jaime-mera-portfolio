Overview

TrueView is a platform used by pit patrollers working on the ground every day. Rio Tinto was concerned that the platform they were building wasn't ensuring the best productivity or the best safety for employees in the field.

I focused on simplifying access to decision-making data, reducing dependency on other teams to collect information, and creating dashboards that help workers consume, understand, and act—faster and more safely.

The Challenge

The core issue was that field teams didn't have fast access to the data they needed to make decisions, and the platform risked overwhelming users with alerts and configuration complexity.

Decision-making data wasn't easily accessible in the field, workers depended on other teams (and themselves) to collect it, priorities and dispatch settings changed frequently, and alert volume could easily become noise.

The Concept

Together with Rio Tinto we ran a series of workshops to define the main pain points, prioritise them, and identify the use cases we would focus on throughout the project. We also defined the design principles that our design system and product designs would support.

The Solution

The solution focused on two things: first, creating an easy and functional login and registration flow. Second, defining dashboard concepts that present data so users can consume it, understand it, and work with it—making daily jobs faster and safer.

Impact

TrueView helped field teams access decision-making data faster, reduced friction in critical flows, and made the platform safer to use under real-world conditions. The dashboard concept and system patterns improved clarity without overwhelming users.
